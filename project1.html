WHAT WE DO

ADVANCING WOMEN'S INITIATIVES

Educating Girls and Women

One of the most challenging tasks is to educate girls and young women making them 100% employable (and in many cases rehabilitation) especially those belong to socio-culturally most backward and economically impoverished communities. It’s equally challenging to convince parents and retaining the mat schools / institutes with the assurance that these young girls are protected from harm and exploitation, hence they need not be given in early marriage!

Secondly; many of them are the first generation learners and have learning deficiencies in some form or the other, meaning they need some special care and coaching at multiple levels especially with language, reasoning, ESL etc, enabling them to complete their schooling at least up to 10th/12th Grade, thus ensuring them a second chance in life to thrive further.

Thirdly; educating girls and women is also a peace mission. It develops women’s collective social intelligence and that helps to mitigate male violence against them with long term goals, making them more productive. Denying equal opportunities and perpetrating violence against girls & women are the root causes of illiteracy, ill-health and inter-generational poverty, since violence damages women’s immunity and productivity both!

Fourthly; the ‘SEEDS’ also educates and counsels young boys and male partners of women and adolescent girls so as to improve their reproductive health, institutional delivery, reduce IMR, mitigate and reduce gender-based or domestic violence against weaker sections and early marriages, altogether this process improves women’s security, health and productivity which in turn benefit the entire communities.

The ‘SEEDS’ has Two Streams of Education Support Systems

A. Formal Education

  1. School Education: Supporting girls for their tuition, meals, cloths, health-care products and counseling etc, enabling them to complete at least school education (10th/12th Grades) which would improve their earning capacity, prevent early marriages, enable better control over their reproductive health, family size and baby-care;
  2. Vocational Health Education: the ‘SEEDS’ manages ‘Nursing & Allied health-care Institutes’ with the clear goals to continue educating and empowering adolescent girls, women and youth of 12th Grader, and making them employable. Till date the students trained by ‘SEEDS’ have secure 100% employments into several prestigious health-care service providers in India and even in Middle-East with better pay. This is indeed one of the most remarkable achievements with the first generation women learners!!

B. Informal Education

  1. Life-skill Development Training: targeting the victims of GBVs, early marriages and economic exploitations, many of whom are illiterate or school drop-outs due to various circumstances say poverty or cultural barriers etc. The ‘SEEDS’ raises local women’s collectives as ‘Advocates and Peace-makers’ and trains them with the lessons on legal and counseling aspects, enabling them to reach out to the victims and mitigate many of the crises locally and amicably which will ensure lasting peace;
  2. Lessons on Food Security, Sustainable Lifestyle and Reproductive Health: locally raised children, youth and housewives are trained with a curriculum-based practices of food productions, nutrition gardening, recycling of household waste to wealth (W2W), water governance, food preservation, and menstrual health & hygiene, holistic health management etc through forming Women’s Parliaments at village level;
  3. Financial Literacy with Vocational Training: The training includes livelihood, small savings schemes, and micro finance with small business support and ensure that women are able to get access to formal banking system. Many of our nursing students are raised with these efforts and are successfully leading their new life with better income and able to educate their kids with formal schooling, and that’s how a transformation has began.
