WHAT WE DO

Supporting Gender-just Thriving Local Communities...

Adopting schools and villages by the 'Nursing & Allied Health-care Institute’ is first of its kind in India, driven by our vision to remove the structural inequalities, especially the ones who can’t do it by otherwise. The ‘SEEDS’ intervenes into three critical areas: education, holistic health and sustainable livelihood with two specific programmatic goals:

First; Developing Market-linked Local Resources: we work hard to develop local community resources such as human capitals (especially youth and women) and local produces, and link them both with the market needs. We ensure youth and women get the necessary skills and access to financial resources as they make up more than half of the population. Though the hungry and sick need immediate attention, yet it’s equally important to alleviate the factors causing recurrent hunger and sickness, with long term solutions, and that’s where functional education plays the crucial role targeting the hard-to-reach and the ones who’re unable to seek help openly due to gender bias and social stigma.

Secondly; Transforming Gender and Power Relations: social structures, norms and values that underpin them, are critical to ending poverty & challenging inequality. Women's participation in transformative leadership, taking control over their legitimate rights with collective actions are the most important drivers of sustained improvement, and a powerful force to reduce inequality. It so happened that majority of those’re living in poverty are women, simply because they’ve fewer resources, lesser power to influence in decision making when compared to men. Most often women get exposed to various forms of violence and exploitation due to their physical indispositions especially expectant and feeding mothers, widow, divorcees and other marital status. Therefore; it’s essential to make women's voices heard at all levels of decision making as it affects their everyday lives.

We strongly believe and promote a Thriving Community with the following basic Necessities...

  • Access to Quality Public Health-care Services;
  • Equal Rights, Rule of Law with Social Security;
  • Universal Quality Education with a value system that promotes equality for all;
  • Inclusive Economy, better and alternative livelihood with Food Security;
  • Environment – clean air and water with adequate greenery that promotes holistic health;

Ensuring Gender-just Thriving Local Communities needs both actions: a preventive – male attitude towards women and relationship with all others; and progressive – a sustainable community life style. People will be healthy if the planet is healthy since both are intrinsically connected and to achieve it, requires responsible collective actions that start with the development of ‘human social capital’ powered by education with a value system that promotes non-violence and equality before law. It’s equally important the way we build and the path it takes to develop our communities have a profound effect on our physical and mental health...
