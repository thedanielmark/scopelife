RELEVANCE & IMPACT OF ‘SCOPE’

The ‘Society for Community Organisation & Peoples Education’ – (SCOPE) is a -for-Profit Centre for ensuring Social Justice with Education, Community-based Healthcare Management, Local Economic Development and Rehabilitation of the victims with a wholistic approach’. The ‘SCOPE’ has five major areas of targeted interventions: ‘Holistic Development of Children; Advancing Initiatives for Girls & Women; Community-based Holistic Health Management; Re-building Local Economy; and Good Governance with Gender Justice’.

Our journey began as ‘Community-based Health-care Service Provider & Rehabilitation’ for the persons affected with Leprosy, TB, and HIV/AIDS including children. We were faced with Two Major Social Challenges: the ‘Stigma and Community Norms’ against these sufferers, forcing them to live in seclusion amidst life threatening situations barring them from public life, common water resources, labour market and socio-cultural activities; and Secondly; the ‘Social Injustices against the Children and Women’ belonging to Tribal and socio-economically most marginalized communities without education, food and shelter forcing them to resort to unsafe economic migration that often ends up in human trafficking especially girls and women become victims of economic & sexual exploitations aggravating the causes of HIV/AIDS. Back at home they face gender discrimination, early marriage, domestic & gender-based violence etc…are the direct results of social injustices with gender imbalance for which there’s a high prevalence of these diseases, hunger and malnutrition in the District.


The lessons we’ve learnt from the above facts are that without education especially for girls, improving local economy and change of social norms through institutional reformation, no amount of efforts can contain these diseases, hunger, early marriage, human trafficking in person, exploitations and violence in multiple forms! For it’s proven that an extra year of girls’ education reduces poverty by 39% and more, apart from controlling GBVs, early marriage, family size and women’s reproductive health problem. It further enables their participation in local governance & decision making power to bring in the changes in community norms and gender balancing…towards achieving at least 6 of 17 SDGs by 2030.


Thus, ‘SCOPE’ was born with the missions to mobilize and empower communities at various levels leading to mass awareness and change of social behaviours against stigma, discrimination and injustices primarily through health-care, education and skill development. The ‘SCOPE’ reaches-out to the affected victims of the infectious diseases through ‘Active Search, Mass Education & Treatment (ASET), and Rehabilitation with Alternative Livelihood’ and implements a Comprehensive Education Program for the children especially girls; Women’s Reproductive & Holistic Health Management; Micro Finance; Legal and Financial Literacy for Adults; and Skill Development that supports sustainable food productions with water governance and alternative livelihood for the disabled victims, women and farmers of the most marginalized in the District. Scope & Achievements

Till date the ‘SCOPE’ has Cured and Rehabilitated more than 617 Leprosy patients, Educated 3,521 Children, 76 Bi-cycles for Girls to go to schools and Rehabilitated 18,500 Women with Skill Development and Micro Finance for Livelihood Support; and Trained 7,321 Farmers with Sustainable Food Productions and Water Governance in Chittoor District of Andhra Pradesh. The ‘SCOPE’ was registered in the year 2008 as charitable society in A.P and subsequently with the Govt. of India with all legal formalities. The ‘SCOPE’ has got recognitions from community leaders and the State as a Credible Centre for Education, Rehabilitation and a Health-care Service Provider.

Who we are?

We’re a group of philanthropists made up of renowned community leaders, doctors and a social engineer, working under the active leadership of Mr. Parthipa and Mrs. Prameela as Founders. The founder Mr. Parthipa, himself is a victim of stigma, as he hails from Leprosy affected family and that has led to the creation of ‘SCOPE’! The ‘SCOPE’ has re-emerged to challenge the community-social behavior with several innovative initiatives which are essential to mitigate stigma and ensure social justice under the new leadership of Dr. Arup K. a Social Engineer and an Author, who plays a key role in raising & training children, youth and women as ‘Ambassadors of Peace & Holistic Health Management’ who play a crucial role at the grass-root levels as preventive measures against several communicable diseases and social injustices. What we do?

A.Community Health & Rehabilitation Program:

  • Prevention of Disability: early diagnosis is the most important factor for the persons affected by Leprosy through ‘ASET’ - (Active Search, Education & Treatment);
  • Rehabilitation of the Treated Cases of Leprosy, HIV/AIDS and TB belonging to Socio-economic marginalized communities thru Skill Development for alternative livelihood;
