TRANSFORMING HEALTH EDUCATION FROM ‘SICK-CARE TO HOLISTIC-CARE’ BY EDUCATING GIRLS & WOMEN BEYOND THE REALM OF CLASS ROOM...

“…mere textbook information can’t just get converted into a transformative powerhouse of knowledge without the lessons of life. Educating girls unlocks the lessons of life that creates healthy communities for us to live a healthy life…!”

- Dr. Arup K: Director – Holistic Health & Community Mobilization.

The ultimate goal of ‘SEEDS’s community interventions with Patient-care Health Education and Services is to develop ‘Collective Human Social Capitals with an Outstanding Value System’. The dual objectives of ‘SEEDS’s nursing education targeting young girls and boys are to enable them hundred percent employable and restoration of the public health. It’s far beyond the teaching of formal patient-care health education curriculum prescribed by the govt, but also to ensure reaching the ‘Concept and Practices of Holistic Health Management’ - (HHM) to the household levels. Since managing public health begins at individual homes and is directly proportional to the level of women being part of it, and therefore, educating girls becomes an imperative with no exception! The formal patient-care health education supported by ‘HHM Curriculum as Lessons of Life’ achieved beyond academies, enable all our trained professionals to become the beacons of hope, health and healing of body and mind for the millions with their personal lives, who naturally engage, influence and transform community public health and delivery system. And that’s the kind of holistic health-care education and values we try our best to provide.

One of the most challenging tasks is to educate adolescent girls and women especially the first generation learners belonging to the most backward communities and enable them hundred percent employable and to get absorbed by some of the most prestigious health-care institutes (hospitals) and public health-care service providers. It makes us truly happy and humble with the sense that our labour is not going in vein!

This is the first place in the history of India, that Nursing and Allied Health-care Institutes adopt schools and villages in the Districts as a strategic intervention methodology, creating pace for these professionals to personally experiment the practices of ‘HHM’ among school students and helping them to practice the ‘HHM’ in their homes especially the victims of socio-cultural and religious stigma and are unable to seek help openly. In due process, the students, community members and health-care professionals together gain far more in-depth knowledge on public health restoration and eventually develop ‘Collective Human Social Intelligence’- (CHSI). The ‘CHSI’ further develops an ‘Aglorithm’ on the pattern of out-break of a disease and how the communities react to it. This Aglorithm as lessons of life, can predict and prevent communities from any future major health catastrophe well in advance …all these make our trained health-care professionals more competitive, giving them an edge over others in the industry!
