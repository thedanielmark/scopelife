WHAT WE DO

‘Community Health Intervention’ and raising the ‘Ambassadors of Holistic Health Management’- (AHHM) who enhance reaching the ‘Concept and Practices of Holistic Health Management’ - (HHM) to the Household levels...

Responding to the several new challenges towards achieving sustainable development goals require solutions that leverage human-capitals and a policy level reformation, especially in a country to the ‘hard to reach population’ plagued with illiteracy, women and children’s ill-health, socio-cultural norms and stigma and gender-based discrimination, among other factors such as access to affordable healthcare products and sustainable livelihood, because of which people can’t enjoy a healthy life and contribute meaningfully to the community wellbeing, since all these’re intertwined and influence development outcomes. The urgent need of raising the ‘Ambassadors of Holistic Health’ and reaching the ‘Practices of Holistic Health Management- (HHM) to the household level with a new vision from ‘Sick-care to Holistic-care’, which can be practiced by the empowered children, adolescent girls and women. When the earning members and especially mothers fall sick, it only aggravates economic burden affecting entire family especially the babies.

The ‘SEEDS’ implements programs to restore community health, and curriculum-based ‘HHM’ by forming Children and Women’s Parliaments (CPAN) at schools and villages. Our major endeavor is to improve the quality of Public Health Services and strengthen the Delivery System. These are some of the innovative initiatives are to achieve Sustainable Development Goals 2030.

Through ‘CPAN’ the ‘SEEDS’ has been trying to ensure…

‘Institutional Reformation’ - is the vital step that enables girls and women to overcome multiple huddles and barriers of socio-cultural norms and religious institutions. The ‘SEEDS’ takes these challenges head on while working with the community institutes to educating leaders, youth, decision makers, common citizens and victims of injustices, ensuring that the girls and women have equal access to education, health and nutrition on par with their male counter parts...

Developing Resilient Communities against Social Evils, Ill-health & Climate Change through forming ‘Women’s Parliaments’ - (WPAN) –adopting communities by a Nursing Institute anywhere in India is unheard of, but that’s how ‘SEEDS’ mobilizes and educates the illiterates, school drop-out girls and housewives and forms them into ‘parliaments at the village levels’, bringing them on a platform with a shared vision and common goals enabling them to discuss issues openly, concerning the challenges and opportunities, and empowers them with knowledge and skills they need, to find solutions for much of their challenges by themselves locally. In the due process they develop resilience against exploitations and injustices. As a result many of the women are able to place themselves among the decision makers, find ways to sustainable use of natural resources, water governance and food productions, alternative livelihood and mitigate the effects of climate change disproportionately affecting them…to name a few.
